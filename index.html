About Me

I am Xuan Wang. My research focuses on multimodal machine learning, wearable sensing, rehabilitation assessment, fall risk prediction, and gait disorder detection.

I work on combining wearable sensor signals, 3D skeletal motion data, and interpretable machine learning methods to support intelligent health assessment for aging and healthcare applications.

My recent work has appeared in IEEE Journal of Biomedical and Health Informatics, BMC Geriatrics, and BMC Public Health.

Research Interests

Selected Projects

Wearable Sensor-based Freezing of Gait Detection in Parkinson's Disease. Developed a generalized monitoring framework that integrates dynamic latent variables with threshold-based and statistical process control methods for personalized anomaly detection.

Machine Learning for Fall Risk Assessment. Built sensor-driven prediction models for community-dwelling older adults using multifaceted feature extraction, ensemble learning, and motion analysis from wearable and skeletal data.
